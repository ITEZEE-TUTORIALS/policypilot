# PolicyPilot Speaker Notes

## Slide 1
- Open with a calm, confident tone.
- Make it clear this is a grounded assistant, not a flashy AI demo.

## Slide 2
- Emphasize that the bottleneck is finding the answer, not producing text.
- Keep the pain point relatable to anyone who has searched a handbook.

## Slide 3
- Give the one-sentence RAG definition.
- Use the librarian analogy if the room needs it.

## Slide 4
- Walk the pipeline left to right.
- Do not over-explain each stage; the goal is to show the shape of the system.

## Slide 5
- Rust is the trust layer.
- Mention safety and deployment simplicity as the practical reasons it matters.

## Slide 6
- RIG is the coordination layer.
- Use the conductor metaphor to explain orchestration without overcomplicating it.

## Slide 7
- Read the demo question out loud.
- Pause before revealing the answer so the retrieval step feels meaningful.

## Slide 8
- Point to the excerpt first, then the final answer.
- The audience should see the grounding happen in front of them.

## Slide 9
- The value slide should feel business-friendly.
- Talk about trust, speed, and fewer mistakes.

## Slide 10
- End on the thesis: good RAG systems do not replace facts, they surface them.
- Leave the room with the line about looking first, then answering.
